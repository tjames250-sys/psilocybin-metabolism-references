Purpose of This Project

This project explores the biochemical pathways through which psilocybin-containing mushrooms may influence neuroplasticity, neuroprotection, and brain health.

Current evidence suggests that psilocin, the active metabolite of psilocybin, can activate signaling pathways associated with synaptogenesis, dendritic spine formation, and adaptive neural rewiring. Additional compounds present within mushrooms, including β-carbolines and other indole alkaloids, may contribute to these effects through mechanisms that remain under active investigation.

This website separates established findings from emerging research questions and serves as a companion reference for the educational brochure.
