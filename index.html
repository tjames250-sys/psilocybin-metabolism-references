Core Question

How might psilocybin support recovery by influencing mood, behavior change, and neuroplasticity?

Recovery Context

Recovery sits at the intersection of neuroplasticity, mood, behavior change, social support, and meaning-making. The strongest claims come from human research, while animal studies and mechanism papers help point toward future questions.

Clinical signal

Psilocybin and behavior change

Human studies have explored psilocybin-assisted therapy for alcohol use disorder and tobacco addiction.

Mood overlap

Depression and relapse risk

Mood research matters because depression, hopelessness, craving, and relapse vulnerability can reinforce one another.

Preclinical signal

4-HIAA and conditioning

A 2025 mouse study on methamphetamine-conditioned place preference points toward future questions about learned reward and reconditioning.

Purpose

This site supports an educational brochure and a growing synthesis of psilocybin's place in recovery research, including neuroplasticity mechanisms, mushroom chemistry, cultural context, and open questions for future study. It is designed as a starting point: simple on the surface, with deeper pages for readers who want the evidence, mechanisms, and context behind the brochure.

Educational note: Personal health context matters. Psychedelics, MAOIs, ibogaine, psychiatric medications, and substance-use treatment decisions are best discussed with qualified clinicians.
