A Living Source Trail

This project is designed to remain open to correction. Researchers, clinicians, recovery staff, advocates, source authors, and careful readers are invited to identify errors, recommend original sources, share emerging work, or suggest questions that deserve closer study.

Editorial relationship: This is an independent educational synthesis. Citation indicates that a source informed the project; it does not imply that an author, institution, or publisher endorses the synthesis.

What Helps Most

  • The page or diagram your message concerns.
  • A DOI, official record, trial identifier, or stable source URL when available.
  • Whether the evidence is human clinical, animal, cellular, mechanistic, policy, historical, cultural, or lived experience.
  • A short explanation of what should be corrected, added, clarified, or investigated.
Privacy note: Please send source links rather than unexpected attachments, and do not send personal medical records or requests for individualized treatment advice. Submissions are reviewed manually and are not published automatically.
